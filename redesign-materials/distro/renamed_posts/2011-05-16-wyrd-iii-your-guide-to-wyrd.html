From the carnal voyeurism of Aaron Levin:
We are five days until Wyrd III, so I figured it was time to unleash this beast of a playlist. Below is the proverbial "sip of the juice." A sprinkling of audible treats from each artist playing Wyrd III. However, like all great maps, the details are not exact. Your final destination is only truly experienced by traveling. We can't wait to see you at Wyrd III. (Full Playlist. Individual details below!)
Long, Long, Long (Halifax, NS) file-under: Haligonia why we love them: link Halifax's hypernetic double-pop quarter ceaselessly pushes all the limits of catch. They're deep-ear diving into a power-pop tornado, channeling chaotic waves into torrential harmonies and six-string rhythmania. Definitely the most exciting thing happening east of Montreal! [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/01-Ponytail-Ambition.mp3|titles=- Ponytail Ambition] Wyrd Visions (Toronto, ON) file-under: slooooooow why we love them: link They call him the bog lord for good reason. Meditative journey's through finger-picked oblivion, Wyrd Visions holds the title for best acoustic Black Metal cover and longest song featuring a Canadian signing in Swedish. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/03-freezing-moon.mp3|titles=Wyrd Visions - Freezing Moon] Red Mass (Montreal, QC) file-under: weird.punk why we love them: link Legendary collective from Montreal birthed from the ashes of the great CPC Gangbangs. Blistering walls of psychedelic punk with terrifying levels of Danzigian vocal delay and gyrating bass-lines. Hands down one of the best phenomena currently recordable. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/09/01-terrorizer.mp3|titles=Red Mass - Terrorizer] GOBBLE GOBBLE (Edmonton, AB) file-under: neon explosion why we love them: link Immutable neon barnacles bursting infinite within every spurt of creative marvel. GOBBLE GOBBLE are without a doubt the most singular dance sensation to be experienced by humankind. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/01-Wrinklecarver.mp3|titles=GOBBLE GOBBLE - Wrinklecarver] Famines (Edmonton, AB) file-under: Bezerker garage. why we love them: link Berserker garage duo Famines lay a relentless drum-pattern rampage paired with split-guitar mayhem. Intenseintenseintenseintenseintense. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/06/01-A1-Syllables.mp3|titles=Famines - Syllables] Hobo Cubes (Montreal, QC) file-under: NASA why we love them: link Existing within the submerged foldable of a hyper dimension psychedelic jungle, Hobo Cubes capture the untamed reverberations emanating from the core of unknowable places. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/binary/hobocubes-onde.mp3|titles=Hobo Cubes - Onde Astrali (w/ Bernardino Femminielli)] Dirty Beaches (Vancouver, BC) file-under: Smoke why we love them: link Dirty Beaches' low-bit explorations through reverb hell have reached an all-time high on the synesthesia scale. Bubbling, smokey pulses of burnt low-end colour his spectrum of hazy crooning and rolling bass lines. Rockabilly for an entirely new generation of smoke. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/binary/dirtybeaches-16.mp3|titles=Dirty Beaches - 16 Coaches Long Akai Tape] Femminielli (Montreal, QC) file-under: SEX why we love them: link Bernardino Femminielli has completed his transformation from a levitating shaman to the sexiest man in subterranean showbiz. Whispering, crooning, and commanding, Femminielli's signature lusty leer follows in the Gainsbourg tradition of aged lechery set against an alien chorus of skin-chilling pulsating synths. SEXY. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/binary/femminielli-estados.mp3|titles=Femminielli - Estados Alterados] Tonstartssbandht (Montreal, QC) file-under: druid brotherhood why we love them: link With a choralic thunder unmatched by any stream of sinusoids, Tonstartssbandht have crossed every genre-boundary, bringing gifts from musical forms yet to be imagined. From a psychedelic accapella to throws of droning guitar wrenchery, Tonstartssbandht are not for the weak of heart. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/binary/Weird_Canada-Tonstartssbandht-Our_Garden.mp3|titles=Tonstartssbandht - Hymn Our Garden] Feral Children (Saskatoon, SK) file-under: prairie psych why we love them: link A forward-thinking, genre-hybridization of trends existing in the ethers of our musical consciousness. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/06-safe-language.mp3|titles=Feral Children - Safe Language] Silver Dapple (Calgary, AB // Montreal, QC) file-under: 90s adora-pop why we love them: link In our infinite adoration of mellotude emerges a trio so pure the mere letters that spill their name tell tall tales of a majestic sky. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/SILVER-DAPPLE-Slip-Stitch.mp3|titles=Silver Dapple - Slip Stitch] Ketamines (Lethbridge, AB) file-under: cocaine why we love them: link Line, by line, by line. Line, by line, by line. It's my world. Pretty much the greatest pop song ever. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Ketamines-Line-by-Line.mp3|titles=Ketamines - Line by Line] Velvet Chrome (Montreal, QC) file-under: dual-masked readymades! why we love them: link Desolation, litter, and decrepit rides breaking through our mourning light; a psychedelic vision from oracles and witches brewed for your stereo. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/10/01-Drowning-in-Space.mp3] Manic Attracts (Vancouver, BC) file-under: grippin' and flipping' garage-punk why we love them: link Drenched in the high-fi red-line popitude we're only capable of conjuring in the depths of a creative zenith, Manic Attracts multiply this intense wave of grage-punk into a veritable factory of sound. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Manic-Attracts-Death-Of-Your-Heart.mp3] Role Mach (Vancouver, BC) file-under: There's 9 people in this band. why we love them: link There are nine people in this band, but the most important part is the full horn section. Horn-rock is back. Throw away your Grand Funk Railroad albums and get ready to embrace the new wave of fonk. [audio:http://weirdcanada.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Role-Mach-black-manhattan.mp3]
