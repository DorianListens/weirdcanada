From brotherly diary entries of Pam Haasen:
This is a diary entry from my cousin’s recollection of her first acid trip. I think it applies here: Crying will do no good. I am on the brink of everything. Tears well up and overwhelming joy tingles through my body like a hacky sack teeming with ants and funny bones. Moving my fingers is the most graceful thing in the world and a stream underneath IT ALL sends a wake that my brain can wind surf on. I’m laughing now like I’ve been hypnotized and my memory is in shock. I wish my hair was a little longer, though.
