King-Beezz
Found and Lost b/w Now
(Quality)
Edmonton, AB
Originally Released: 1966
From the Lost and Found of Aaron Levin: The King-Beezz's third single is the toughest polyvinyl artifact from Edmonton. "Now"'s snarling, wrangled guitar leads, put-me-down harmonies, and screaming, postured ad-libs trash every punk archetype in the purest teenage pursuit of attitude. On the A-Side, "Found and Lost"'s jangly, loner lament, bass-walkery and bedroom percussion craft a brilliant bizarro pop-psych excursion into the recesses of vintage fringe culture. Just ridiculous stuff. 'Twas the bees-knees (!) finding a copy complete with the glorious Quality company sleeve. A full history of the King-Beezz can be found here.
